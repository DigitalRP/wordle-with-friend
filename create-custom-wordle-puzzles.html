Before beginning a custom Wordle with friends session, it is wise to set clear rules and expectations. Discuss how hints will be used, if any specific word categories are allowed, and what constitutes a valid win. This upfront discussion ensures fair play and reduces confusion.

Troubleshooting Common Issues When Creating Puzzles

While creating custom Wordle puzzles is generally straightforward, users might occasionally encounter minor issues. Knowing how to troubleshoot these common problems ensures a seamless experience. This allows you to quickly resolve issues and get back to enjoying Wordle with friends without delay.

Most problems are easily fixable and relate to input errors or sharing mechanisms. Our tool is designed for simplicity, but a quick check of your inputs can often solve any snags. This section helps you identify and fix typical challenges you might face.

Word Not Accepted?

If your custom Wordle word is not accepted, check the input requirements. Ensure the word consists only of alphabetic characters and matches the selected length. Our tool validates these aspects to provide a playable puzzle for others.

Double-check for any accidental spaces or special characters. Confirm the word's length matches the numeric input value. Correcting these details usually resolves the issue, allowing you to generate your puzzle successfully and share it with your Wordle with friends.

Sharing Link Problems?

Should you encounter issues with the sharing link, first confirm that the puzzle was successfully created and the link appeared. Sometimes, network issues can briefly affect link generation or loading. Try regenerating the puzzle or refreshing the page if the link does not appear as expected.

Ensure you are copying the entire link. If sharing via a messaging app, confirm it is not truncating the URL. Most platforms handle long URLs well, but a complete copy is essential for friends to play online correctly.
